▶ 📖 About Me

🎓 I am Jiajie Li, a PhD candidate based in Wuhan, with research focusing on computer vision and spatiotemporal image processing. I specialize in image spatiotemporal reconstruction, conducting algorithm research on core vision tasks including image inpainting and resolution enhancement. I am currently developing a unified research framework that integrates spatial features, temporal dependencies, and multi-source data. With solid hands-on experience in image processing and deep learning implementation, I hold a strong passion for exploring cutting-edge advances in computer vision.

🌱 Coming from an interdisciplinary background in environmental ecology, remote sensing, and mathematical statistics, I have long been enthusiastic about cross-domain integration research in computer vision. My research journey began with spatial data analysis and temporal modeling practices. Starting from traditional image processing and statistical analysis methods, I have progressively delved into deep learning-driven image reconstruction and multi-source information fusion. This cross-disciplinary growth has laid a solid mathematical foundation for me, and fostered my mindset of approaching vision problems from real-world scenarios. I highly value every opportunity for technical exploration and collaborative research.

💻 I always believe in the power of continuous learning and sincere collaboration. Outside of research, I enjoy sports and music to stay energized. I keep a sustained focus and strong curiosity for frontier directions including image and video processing and multimodal large models. I am open to exchanging technical insights and sharing practical experience, and look forward to collaborating with peers from diverse backgrounds. Feel free to reach out to me anytime .it would be my great pleasure to have academic discussions, technical exchanges, or project cooperation with you.
